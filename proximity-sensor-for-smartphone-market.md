<p><p>Proximity sensors for smartphones come in two main types: Optical Displacement Sensors and Ultrasonic Displacement Sensors. Optical sensors work by emitting a beam of infrared light and measuring the reflection off an object to determine distance. Ultrasonic sensors, on the other hand, use sound waves to calculate proximity. Both types of sensors are widely used in smartphones for features like automatic screen brightness adjustments, touch detection, and gesture recognition. They play a crucial role in enhancing user experience and improving device functionality.</p></p>
