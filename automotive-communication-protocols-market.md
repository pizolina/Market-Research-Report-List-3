<p><p>The Automotive Communication Protocols market encompasses various types of protocols used in vehicles. LIN (Local Interconnect Network) is a low-cost, single-wire network for communication between automotive components. CAN (Controller Area Network) is a standard protocol for high-speed communication between different modules in the vehicle. FlexRay is a deterministic, fault-tolerant protocol used in safety-critical applications. MOST (Media Oriented Systems Transport) facilitates high-speed multimedia communication within the vehicle. Ethernet, a widely used protocol, enables fast and reliable communication for advanced applications in modern vehicles. These protocols play vital roles in establishing efficient communication networks within automobiles.</p></p>
