<p><p>Pellet fuel market types include industrial waste and co-products, which are materials left over from manufacturing processes. Food waste pellets are made from discarded food items. Agricultural residues are byproducts from crops such as corn stalks and sugar cane bagasse. Energy crops like switchgrass and willow trees are grown specifically for fuel production. Virgin lumber pellets are created from freshly cut timber. Each type of pellet fuel offers a sustainable alternative to traditional fossil fuels, reducing waste and environmental impact.</p></p>
