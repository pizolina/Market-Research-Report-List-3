<p><p>Protein purification is a crucial process in biotechnology and research, wherein proteins are separated from impurities to obtain pure, high-quality samples. Several methods are employed for this purpose. Ultrafiltration uses membranes to separate proteins based on size. Precipitation involves adding chemicals to precipitate proteins and remove impurities by centrifugation. Centrifugation separates proteins based on density and size using high-speed spinning. Chromatography uses stationary and mobile phases to separate proteins based on their distinct properties. Electrophoresis separates proteins based on size and charge using an electric field. Other methods include crystallization and solvent extraction.</p></p>
