<p><p>Residential cooking grills refer to outdoor cooking appliances used for grilling food at home. There are three main types of residential cooking grills: gas cooking grills, charcoal cooking grills, and electric cooking grills. Gas cooking grills are fueled by propane or natural gas, offering convenience and easy temperature control. Charcoal cooking grills use charcoal briquettes or lump charcoal, providing a traditional smoky flavor but requiring more time and effort. Electric cooking grills run on electricity, offering ease of use and no need for fuel, but may sacrifice the authentic BBQ taste.</p></p>
