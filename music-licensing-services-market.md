<p><p>The music licensing services market consists of various types, including print, public performance, and others. Print licensing involves obtaining the rights to reproduce and distribute sheet music or lyrics. Public performance licensing allows businesses and venues to play music for public consumption, such as in restaurants, stores, or concerts. Other forms of licensing may include synchronization rights for music used in films, commercials, or online videos, and mechanical rights for reproducing music on CDs or digital platforms. These types of licensing ensure that music creators are properly compensated for the use of their work in different contexts.</p></p>
