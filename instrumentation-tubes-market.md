<p><p>Instrumentation tubes are commonly found in various industrial applications, and they are available in two main types - seamless tubes and welded tubes. Seamless tubes are produced without any welding seam, resulting in a smoother surface and higher pressure resistance. Welded tubes, on the other hand, are created by welding together two ends of a strip of metal, making them more cost-effective but slightly less durable compared to seamless tubes. Both types of tubes play a crucial role in ensuring the efficient and reliable operation of instrumentation systems.</p></p>
