<p><p>The meal fibers market consists of two main types: soluble fiber and insoluble fiber. Soluble fiber dissolves in water and forms a gel-like substance, which helps regulate blood sugar levels and lower cholesterol. It is found in foods like oats, barley, and legumes. On the other hand, insoluble fiber does not dissolve in water and adds bulk to the digestive system, aiding in regular bowel movements. It is commonly found in whole grains, vegetables, and nuts. Both types of fiber play a crucial role in maintaining a healthy digestive system and overall well-being.</p></p>
